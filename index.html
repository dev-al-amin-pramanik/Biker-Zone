Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

To determine if your helmet needs replacement, check its age, as most helmets have a lifespan of 3-5 years from the manufacture date (found inside the helmet). Inspect for visible damage like cracks, dents, or a compromised inner liner, and replace the helmet if it's been involved in a crash or drop, even without visible damage. Additionally, worn padding, loose straps, or fading from UV exposure indicate it may no longer provide adequate protection and should be replaced.

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

If your bike has been in storage, it’s important to inspect it thoroughly before riding to ensure it’s safe. Check the tires for cracks, flat spots, or low pressure, and inflate them to the recommended PSI. Inspect the brakes to ensure they’re functioning properly and check for any rust on the chain, gears, or frame. Lubricate the chain and test the gears to ensure smooth shifting. Look for signs of leaks or damage in the fuel system (if applicable) and check that all lights and signals are working. If you’re unsure or the bike has been in storage for a long time, consider having it serviced by a professional before riding.

What rules should I follow when riding my bike?

When riding your bike, always wear a helmet and protective gear, obey traffic laws, and ride in the same direction as traffic. Ensure you're visible by using lights, reflectors, and bright clothing, especially at night. Use hand signals to communicate turns or stops, stay alert to your surroundings, and avoid distractions like using your phone. Maintain a safe distance from vehicles and follow speed limits, prioritizing your safety and that of others on the road.
